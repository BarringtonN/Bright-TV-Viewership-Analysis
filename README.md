BrightTV Viewership Analytics and Growth Strategy

This project delivers a data driven analysis of BrightTV’s user base and content consumption patterns, with a focus on translating insights into measurable business growth. The work combines data analysis, behavioural segmentation, and strategic recommendations to support subscription growth and customer value optimisation.

Problem Statement

BrightTV’s CEO set a clear objective to grow the subscription base within the current financial year. The challenge was to identify the key drivers of viewership, uncover gaps in engagement, and recommend scalable initiatives that improve both acquisition and retention.

Dataset and Approach

The dataset consists of session level viewing records, where each row represents a user session with associated attributes such as time, channel, and user demographics. This structure enables analysis of both frequency of use and depth of engagement.The analysis was conducted across five core dimensions: geographic distribution, demographic segmentation, temporal consumption patterns, content performance, and engagement behaviour.

Key Analytical Insights

The platform has a total audience of 9,982 viewers, with a strong geographic concentration. Gauteng alone contributes 3,751 viewers, representing 37.6% of the total base, while the Western Cape adds a further 18.7%. Together, these two regions account for approximately 56% of all users, indicating a highly centralised market footprint. In contrast, provinces such as Northern Cape contribute as little as 234 users, highlighting a clear expansion opportunity.

Demographic analysis reveals a significant gender imbalance. Approximately 90% of total viewing is driven by male users, leaving female participation below 10%. This represents one of the largest structural gaps in the platform and a major opportunity for targeted growth.

Age segmentation shows that Young Adults aged 25 to 34 account for 4,250 users, or roughly 43% of the total audience. When combined with the broader 18 to 44 segment, this group contributes approximately 83% of total consumption, making it the primary monetisation cohort.

Temporal analysis highlights strong weekday dominance, with 70.3% of viewing occurring during weekdays. Monday records only 980 viewers, which is 41% lower than the Saturday peak of 1,660 viewers. This indicates clear underperformance at the start and end of the week, where targeted programming can drive incremental engagement.

Time of day analysis reveals a shift away from traditional viewing patterns. Afternoon viewing peaks at 2,450 users, exceeding Prime Time, which reaches 1,850 users. This suggests changing consumption habits, likely influenced by flexible work patterns and mobile streaming behaviour.

Content performance analysis confirms that live sports are the primary driver of both acquisition and engagement. SuperSport Live Events generate more than 1,600 unique viewers, while the ICC Cricket World Cup contributes nearly 1.5 million minutes of watch time, making it the strongest duration anchor on the platform.

Engagement analysis follows a power law distribution. A small group of high frequency users generates over 1.1 million minutes of total engagement, while the majority of users remain in a low activity segment with fewer than 100 sessions. This indicates a large opportunity to convert casual users into habitual viewers.

Service reliability analysis shows critical operational risks. Transmission breaks peak at 25 interruptions on Tuesdays and 18 on Saturdays, both of which are high traffic days. These failures directly impact user experience and increase churn risk, particularly during peak sports events.

Business Impact and Recommendations

The analysis translates into clear, high impact interventions.Expanding into underserved provinces through tiered pricing and localised marketing can unlock a new segment of price sensitive users. Even a modest penetration increase in low performing regions could significantly increase the total subscriber base.Addressing the gender gap by increasing female focused content has the potential to grow engagement by up to 10% or more, given the current imbalance.

Leveraging sports as the primary acquisition channel provides a proven growth lever. Maintaining and expanding access to live sports content is likely to yield the highest return on investment.Improving engagement among low activity users through personalised recommendations and behavioural nudges can increase session frequency and total watch time.
Strengthening platform reliability, particularly on peak days, is critical to reducing churn and maintaining user trust.
